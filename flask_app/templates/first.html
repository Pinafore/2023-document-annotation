Task Instructions

We have a set of uncategorized US congressional bill documents in English, which cover a variety of subjects. Now we need to create labels for those documents to group them into different categories. Your task is to spend the next 60 minutes exploring the documents and creating category labels that make sense to you.

Please note that this is not a summarization task, and we kindly request that you refrain from providing summaries for the passages. Instead, focus on creating meaningful and general labels that can be applied to multiple passages, ensuring that they are categorized effectively

For this study you will be using an AI-assisted tool that helps humans label a collection of text passages with categories, or themes, of interest. You will be shown passages from the collection of congressional bills, alongside other potentially helpful information, such as cluster keywords generated by the AI-assisted tool from the content of the entire document collection to characterize the theme (by grouping unique words?) of the passage.

Additionally, there will be a 'highlight' feature to emphasize important keywords that the AI-assisted tool identified in each document. Participants will be required to come up with appropriate labels for the presented passages, either by selecting from existing labels or creating new ones. If you like, you can use these cluster keywords to help guide your labeling, or are free to use your own. A clear definition of a 'label' is provided for reference below.

Throughout the study, the AI-assisted tool will also recommend documents to label, based on your past labeling decisions. Alternatively, you can choose any other document not recommended by the AI-assisted tool in the main Documents tab. The progress of work can be tracked in the 'view/edit response' section, where participants can modify their created or assigned labels as needed. Further instructions will be provided in a demo on the following screens.

Definitions:

Label: A label for a passage can consist of a single word or a phrase

Example labels: health, health insurance, child health plans…

Example instance for your task -

Top Cluster Keywords: plan, implementation, local, changes, progress, process, data, measures, time, monitoring

Passage: “There is no perfect science. Develop an adaptive process that allows modification as science advances."

Your created label: Plan Alignment

The total length of the task will not exceed one hour, and the majority of your time will be spent looking at passages and accompanying information in order to annotate passages with labels you create. Please note that for our purposes the quality of labels is just as important as efficiency: please take time to think of what you think are labels that do a good job of summarizing each passage.

If you complete the hour-long session following the instructions you will receive $20 as compensation.

Any suspected fraud or abuse will result in forfeiture of compensation, and that includes not actually completing the task (Fewer than 60 documents unlabeled or low quality labels created).

We will not ask you for any personally identifying information and the annotations will never require any de-anonymizing information from you whatsoever. You must be at least 18 years old in order to participate in this task.
